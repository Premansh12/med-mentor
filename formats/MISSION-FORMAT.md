# MISSION.md format

One page, plain language. Update it as the user's goals evolve — but confirm with them first, and log the change as a learning record.

```markdown
# Mission

## Who I am
Role, stage of training/practice (e.g. "PGY-2 internal medicine resident"),
specialty focus if any.

## What I'm preparing for
The concrete driver: an exam (name + date), a rotation, a recertification,
a specific clinical gap noticed on shift. Be specific — "Step 2 CK, test
date Oct 2026" is more useful than "get better at medicine."

## Why it matters right now
The real stakes: what happens if this isn't learned in time (fail an exam,
unsafe on a rotation, miss a recert deadline). This is what should keep
lessons grounded instead of abstract.

## Current level / starting point
Honest self-assessment or evidence of current level (practice test scores,
attending feedback, self-identified weak areas).

## Constraints
Time available per session, session frequency, format preferences
(e.g. "mostly commuting, audio-first isn't possible, keep lessons short
enough to do in 10 minutes standing up").

## Out of scope (for now)
Anything explicitly deprioritized, so it's not accidentally taught early.

## Revision history
- YYYY-MM-DD: initial mission set
- YYYY-MM-DD: [what changed and why, confirmed with user]
```
