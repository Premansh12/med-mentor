# Mission, Resources, and Wisdom

Read this when: it's the first session, `MISSION.md` is missing/vague, `RESOURCES.md` needs building or updating, or the user asks something that really needs real-world/community contact rather than a lesson.

## Establishing the mission

Every lesson ties back to the mission — the exam, rotation, credential, or clinical gap driving the user's study. If `MISSION.md` is empty or vague, that's the first job, before writing any lesson.

Ask directly: what are they preparing for, on what timeline, and what's the cost of not knowing it (a boards question, a patient encounter, a recert deadline)? That shapes depth vs. breadth for everything that follows. Use `formats/MISSION-FORMAT.md`.

Missions shift as competence grows — e.g. from "pass Step 1" to "be safe on ICU rotation." This is normal. Update `MISSION.md` and log a learning record for the change (`formats/LEARNING-RECORD-FORMAT.md`), but **confirm with the user before changing it**.

## Building RESOURCES.md

Before `RESOURCES.md` is well-populated, prioritize finding current, high-quality sources over teaching from memory — guidelines and dosing get revised, and stale information here is a different kind of risk than in most teaching domains.

Populate it as sources actually get used to ground a lesson, using `formats/RESOURCES-FORMAT.md`:

- **Guidelines/societies** (AHA/ACC, WHO, NICE, ACOG, IDSA, etc.) — prefer these for anything algorithmic or dosing-related
- **Point-of-care references** (UpToDate, DynaMed, StatPearls)
- **Journals** (NEJM, JAMA, Lancet, Cochrane reviews) — for landmark trials or evidence strength
- **Question banks** relevant to the user's target exam
- **Communities** (see below)

If two sources conflict, note the hierarchy this specific user should default to — e.g. board prep often wants the textbook/board answer even where real practice is more nuanced.

## Acquiring wisdom

Wisdom comes from real clinical or exam-room contact — testing knowledge outside the lesson. When a question needs that (e.g. "how does this actually play out with a non-compliant patient," "what do attendings really do here that isn't in the guideline"), answer what you can, but default to pointing toward a **community**: a specialty subreddit, a journal club, a residency cohort, a professional society's discussion board, or an in-person study group, budget permitting.

Prefer high-reputation, clinically-moderated communities over general forums. Log good finds in `RESOURCES.md`. If the user says they don't want a community, respect that and stop suggesting one for the rest of the engagement.
